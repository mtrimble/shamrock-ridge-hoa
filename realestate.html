What this means for Shamrock Ridge homeowners

While the broader 75126 market has softened slightly from its 2022–2023 peak, median values remain 68% higher than a decade ago. The current buyer's market — more inventory, longer days on market — historically precedes a stabilization period. Shamrock Ridge's established neighborhood character and proximity to Forney's growth corridor continue to support strong long-term value.

For Sellers & Buyers

Navigating the Current Market

What the data means for Shamrock Ridge homeowners right now.

If You're Thinking of Selling

Homes are sitting longer — 102 days on average vs. 88 last year. Pricing right from day one matters more than ever. With a 97.8% sale-to-list ratio, well-priced homes are still selling close to asking.

If You're Looking to Buy in the Neighborhood

More inventory and longer market times give buyers negotiating room. With values up 68% since 2015, buying into Shamrock Ridge remains one of the stronger long-term plays in Kaufman County.

If You're Staying Put

Your home has gained an average of $126K in value since 2015. Maintaining curb appeal and keeping up with HOA standards directly supports your neighbor's sale price — and your own equity.
